About Us

Global Shapers Laayoune Hub is on a mission to inspire, empower and equip the next generation of leaders in the southern region of Morocco. We believe that everyone has the potential to make a difference and create positive change in their communities and beyond. Our hub is a vibrant community of young professionals, entrepreneurs, activists, and change-makers who share a common goal: to make the world a better place. Through our various initiatives, projects and events, we provide a platform for young people to develop their skills, network with like-minded individuals, and gain the knowledge and resources they need to make their ideas a reality. From leadership development workshops to community service projects, from entrepreneurship boot camps to cultural exchanges, our hub offers a wide range of opportunities for young people to grow, learn and make an impact. We believe that the future belongs to those who dare to dream big, work hard and persevere in the face of challenges. So if you're ready to unleash your potential and join a community of passionate and driven individuals, come join us at Global Shapers Laayoune Hub. Together, we can make a difference and create a brighter future for all.

Initiatives

Skilling Me Softly

The inaugural edition of the SMS orientation initiative took place from March 26th to 28th and was focused on addressing orientation issues in education, entrepreneurship, and policy-making. With a vision to create the first orientation platform and e-forum that focuses on upskilling entrepreneurs, NGOs, corporations, and students in soft and hard skills, SMS aims to be a trailblazer in this field. The initiative also aims to partner with various civic actors, be it government or private, to foster better inclusion and sustainability. In its first edition, the initiative successfully hosted three workshops on soft skills, featuring three speakers and attracting 131 participants. SMS's commitment to addressing orientation issues in key areas like education, entrepreneurship, and policy-making makes it a crucial initiative for building a sustainable and inclusive future. The SMS orientation platform and e-forum are poised to become an essential resource for individuals and organizations looking to upskill and stay up-to-date on the latest soft and hard skills needed to thrive in today's ever-changing world.

GoMena

The GoMena Project aims to support Moroccan entrepreneurs in developing innovative and scalable tech solutions, promoting self-employment among youths, and creating a qualified talent pool. Through coaching activities and showcasing success stories, the program focuses on developing participants' skills in business, technical capacity building, and lean development methodology approach. The program also aims to create a collaboration between the Moroccan and Israeli entrepreneurial ecosystem and promote awareness about entrepreneurship amongst Moroccan youth. Over five years, the program plans to train 1200 entrepreneurs and create a pipeline of 300 skilled workers for exchange and placement programs, as well as 20 ideated startups for acceleration and investment readiness. The GoMena project is using three major pillars of development methodology to achieve its objectives. The first pillar is business skills development. The project is providing coaching activities on top-tier technological topics and business modeling and idea development. The second pillar is technical capacity building. The project is providing coaching and mentorship by Israeli entrepreneurs, leading talents, mentors, and experts. The third pillar is the lean development methodology approach. The project is building participants' capacity in several areas, including soft skills, technical and entrepreneurial skills, and transferring knowledge from the entrepreneurship nation.

Aji Tfham L SDGs

The "Aji Tfham L SDGs" project is a pioneering national initiative aimed at promoting sustainable development in Morocco through comprehensive coverage and interactive challenges related to the United Nations' Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs). The goal of the project is to present non-intimidating guides, especially to individuals who are new to the topics covered, making the SDGs accessible to a broad audience. The project's target groups include Moroccan Shapers, small to medium size NGOs, and youth, all of whom have the potential to be change-agents in their communities. The initiative aims to boost the necessary efforts and solutions for the SDGs through its comprehensive coverage and interactive challenges. The project's broad demographic range makes it a powerful tool for raising awareness and building inclusive and sustainable initiatives at all levels, contributing to a sustainable future for Morocco and the world. With its innovative approach and targeted impact, the "Aji Tfham L SDGs" project sets an example for national initiatives aimed at promoting sustainable development and achieving the SDGs.

Exolab

Exolab is an innovative educational project between Global Shapers Laayoune and MISI that aims to connect students in Morocco's classrooms with real researchers, scientists, and astronauts on the International Space Station. The project will give students the opportunity to take part in an international experiment on CO2 extraction and the effect of microgravity on plant growth. This experiment will take place simultaneously in various parts of the world, including on the International Space Station. Exolab uses devices equipped with small cameras that take photographs around the clock, allowing students to monitor how their plants are growing in the classroom via an online learning platform. By logging into the platform, students and teachers can access data from the ISS and other locations around the world, including data from other classrooms to which they can compare their experiment. Through Exolab, students can make observations and draw conclusions about what is causing the plant to grow or fail and how that information can be used to solve some of the world's larger problems. MISI and the Scientific Morocco Association are organizing the project with the help of Global Shapers Laayoune Hub in the southern region of Morocco. The project aims to provide space education and outreach in Morocco through the introduction of a hands-on Cubesat farm experiment.
